# **What Startup-as-a-Service Really Looks Like**

*By Jake Ngatchu (Piranha Studios)*

![alt text](image.png)

People throw around “Startup-as-a-Service” like it’s some fancy agency package — a website, a marketing bundle, maybe a bit of design.

That’s not what it is.

The real version is simple:

> **You’re subscribing to a full technical team that builds, runs, and grows your product — the same way a co-founder would — without you having to hire anyone.**

No buzzwords.
No acronyms.
Just reality.

Here’s exactly what it means.

---

# **1. You get a team, not a website**

Traditional agencies sell output:

* a site
* an app
* a feature
* a design

Startup-as-a-Service sells *capability*.

You’re effectively subscribing to:

* engineering
* architecture
* design
* product thinking
* DevOps
* maintenance
* strategy

all in one place.

It replaces the need to hire:

* developers
* designers
* CTO
* project managers
* support staff

Your business gets a **permanent technical arm**.

---

# **2. You focus on the business — the studio handles the technology**

Most founders drown in technical decisions they’re not equipped to make:

* What stack should we use?
* How do we architect this?
* How do we scale?
* Which feature comes next?
* Why is the app down?

Startup-as-a-Service means:

**You never have to think like a developer.**
You only think like a founder.

You say what the business needs.
The technical team handles how to make it happen.

---

# **3. You get a real, modern product stack — not plugins**

This is the difference between a real product studio and a web agency.

A proper Startup-as-a-Service model provides:

* a fast backend
* scalable database
* web and mobile apps
* real-time features
* logging + monitoring
* security
* integrations

At Piranha, the standard stack includes:

* Supabase
* Go backend
* Next.js frontend
* Zustand state
* tRPC
* React Native if needed
* Medusa or Mercur for commerce
* Axiom for logs
* Render for hosting

You’re not getting a template.
You’re getting an actual product ecosystem.

---

# **4. Your product is custom — but built with reusable infrastructure**

This is where the model becomes powerful.

Every project gets:

* custom flows
* custom logic
* custom integrations
* custom admin tools

…but built on top of a core system we’ve already perfected.

So you get:

* custom quality
* template speed
* real scalability
* lower cost
* fewer bugs

This is what lets you build a serious product without spending £200k+.

---

# **5. The technical risk sits with the studio, not the founder**

Traditional agencies deliver and disappear.

Startup-as-a-Service means:

* we monitor your system
* we keep it stable
* we upgrade it
* we patch it
* we make it faster
* we scale it

Your product is someone’s job every month.
Not a forgotten folder.

This means:

* no late-night panics
* no freelancers ghosting
* no “who built this?”
* no rebuilding from scratch
* no mystery codebases

You have a team — not a project.

---

# **6. You get continuous development, not a one-off build**

This is the biggest psychological shift.

A startup is never “done.”
You will always need:

* updates
* new features
* bug fixes
* optimisations
* pivots
* experiments
* feedback loops

Traditional agencies aren’t built for that.
A Startup-as-a-Service studio is.

It turns your product into a **living system** that evolves with your business.

---

# **7. You avoid a quarter-million pounds in salaries**

Here’s the financial truth:

To build a proper digital product, you normally need:

* 1 backend dev
* 1 frontend dev
* 1 designer
* 1 project manager
* 1 DevOps engineer

Total: **£250k–£400k per year**, before equity.

With Startup-as-a-Service, you get the same capability for a tiny fraction of that — because you’re sharing our infrastructure, our stack, our processes, and our team across multiple ventures.

It is **genuinely the most cost-effective way to build a new startup** without burning capital.

---

# **8. This is not agency work — it’s long-term product partnership**

The agency model says:

* “Here’s your thing. Good luck.”

Startup-as-a-Service says:

* “We’re building this with you. Let’s grow it.”

You get:

* roadmap planning
* technical strategy
* monthly development cycles
* support
* performance monitoring
* product thinking
* long-term scalability

Your success = our success.

This is why PHC, GFA, Carmen’s, and Habitaate work under this model.

---

# **9. The real benefit: founders move 10x faster**

With Startup-as-a-Service, founders can:

* launch features in days instead of months
* pivot safely
* scale confidently
* rely on proper infrastructure
* stay focused on customers
* avoid technical distractions
* build multiple ventures in parallel

It turns you from a single-founder operator into a **multi-project owner with a stable technical engine behind you**.

---

# **10. This is the future of building companies**

Hiring is expensive.
Freelancers are unreliable.
Agencies aren’t long-term partners.
Templates don’t scale.

Startup-as-a-Service solves all of that by giving founders:

**A team. A system. A process. A roadmap.
On demand. Every month.**

It’s not a website package.
It’s not a dev contract.
It’s not consultancy.

It’s the modern way to build a startup without destroying your time, money, or mental health.
